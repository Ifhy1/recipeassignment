Classic Spaghetti Bolognese

This rich and hearty Italian classic has been warming dinner tables for generations. Perfect for weeknight comfort or a cozy gathering with friends, its slow-simmered sauce is infused with fragrant herbs, tender minced beef, and sweet tomatoes. Every forkful offers a perfect balance of savory depth and delicate freshness, making it a dish you’ll want to cook again and again.

Recipe Information

Preparation time: 15 minutes

Cooking time: 1 hour

Servings: 4 people

Difficulty: Intermediate

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic, cloves minced
  • 500g minced beef
  • 400g canned chopped tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 300g dried spaghetti

Instructions

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pan over medium heat.
  2. Add the onion and garlic, and sauté until softened.
  3. Stir in the minced beef and cook until browned.
  4. Add the chopped tomatoes, tomato paste, oregano, and basil. Stir well.
  5. Season with salt and pepper, reduce heat, and simmer for 45 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  6. Meanwhile, cook the spaghetti according to the package instructions until al dente.
  7. Drain the spaghetti and toss lightly with a little olive oil to prevent sticking.
  8. Let the sauce rest for 5 minutes to deepen the flavors.
  9. Serve the spaghetti topped generously with the Bolognese sauce.
  10. Garnish with freshly grated Parmesan and fresh basil leaves. Enjoy!

Tips

For extra depth of flavor, add a splash of red wine when adding the tomatoes. You can also sprinkle freshly grated Parmesan on top before serving for a rich finish.

  • Cook the sauce a day ahead — it tastes even better after the flavors have developed overnight.
  • Reserve a cup of pasta cooking water to mix into the sauce for a silkier texture.
  • Use fresh herbs like basil or parsley just before serving for a burst of freshness.
